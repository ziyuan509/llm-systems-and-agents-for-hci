# Evaluation Plan

## Goal

Test whether a delegation-contract interface helps ordinary users govern
privacy-sensitive ambient AI behavior better than black-box or oversight-only
conditions.

## Candidate Conditions

- Condition A: black-box ambient assistant
- Condition B: oversight baseline with step-level privacy markers
- Condition C: delegation-contract interface

## Candidate Tasks

- configure an ambient assistant for a shared studio meeting
- decide how to handle detected bystander speech
- repair a policy after an incorrect memory or disclosure attempt
- explain why the assistant included, excluded, or escalated an action

## Candidate Measures

- boundary comprehension
- intervention success
- repair quality
- calibrated reliance
- perceived control
- mental workload
- perceived privacy and accountability

## Pilot Walkthrough Plan

- recruit 3-5 participants for 20-30 minute walkthroughs
- ask participants to set up the contract, respond to ask-back, and repair policy
- collect task success, hesitation points, misunderstandings, and short quotes
- treat findings as early design insights, not statistical evidence

## Logging Priorities

- when users revise sensing, memory, disclosure, or escalation rules
- what triggers ask-back moments
- whether users choose one-time exceptions or durable policy repair
- whether repair happens at the output level or policy level
- whether users can explain the final audit summary
